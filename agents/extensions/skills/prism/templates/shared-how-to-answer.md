## How to answer

Lead with your assessment, then the evidence and reasoning behind it — where the evidence is inconclusive, say so rather than forcing a verdict. Cite file paths and line numbers from the shared context where they apply, and answer the full question: let your lens change emphasis, not coverage. No preamble, no restating the question, no commentary about the task or how your answer will be used. Keep the body tight — aim for roughly 800 words unless the question genuinely needs more.

End your answer with a digest: a compact, self-contained summary the synthesizer reads first, and at large scale may read in place of your full answer for initial synthesis (it then deep-reads your full answer when your view is pivotal). Put the literal heading `## Digest` on its own line (an H2) — **do not wrap the digest in a code fence** — followed by exactly these four bullets:

- **Position:** your one-sentence answer / recommendation
- **Key reasons:** 1-3 sub-points, each a reason plus its basis
- **Dissent/caveat:** your strongest objection to the likely majority view, or "none"
- **Changes if:** the observable evidence or trigger that would flip your position, or "none"

Do NOT include a self-assigned confidence score (numeric or High/Med/Low) — the synthesizer computes confidence itself and ignores self-scores.
